It's a conversation. Underneath, it's a calculator.

The agent can't invent a result. Each thing it says is the output of a fixed tool reading your file — so the friendly tone never costs you accuracy.

get_phenotype

Reads your status

Finds the variants in your file and resolves your metabolizer type — or returns “not determined.”

check_drug_gene

Maps drug to risk

Pairs a medication with the gene that handles it and the direction of the effect — too little, or too much.

check_phenoconversion

Catches interactions

Flags when another medication you take could blunt an enzyme and shift your effective status.

add_med

Builds your list

Keeps the medications you mention in this session so it can reason across them — locally, never stored remotely.

An agent that knows what it isn't.

The most important thing a health agent does is refuse to overstep. These boundaries are wired in, not improvised.

✓
Explains the mechanismWhy a gene changes what a drug does, in language you can repeat to a clinician.
✕
Never gives a doseNo numbers, no “take this much.” That decision belongs to your prescriber.
✓
Says “not determined” out loudIf your file lacks the deciding variant, it tells you — it never assumes normal.
✕
Never tells you to stop a medIt hands you questions for a professional, not instructions to act on alone.
✓
Shows its workEvery answer is tied to a tool call you can see — no hidden reasoning about your health.
✕
Never uploads your DNAOnly de-identified {gene, status, drug} can be sent — and only to phrase a reply, if you allow it.

Not medical advice. The DoseDNA agent is informational and not a diagnostic device. It does not tell you whether a medication is safe for you and you should not start, stop, or change any medication based on it. When a metabolizer status cannot be determined from your file it is reported as “not determined,” never as normal. Always confirm with a qualified clinician or pharmacist.
